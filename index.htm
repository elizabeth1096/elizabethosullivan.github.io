TRIUMF - Spectrometer for Internal Conversion Electrons (SPICE):
Student Researcher

My main project was developing and commissioning a more cost effective heavy ion detector than that currently in use. I designed a detector that allowed for individual burnt out segments to be removed as required saving the SPICE team upwards of $10, 000.

I wrote a program to automatically cool the SPICE experimental to 77 K that could be controlled remotely. This allowed researchers to begin, end, and monitor the cooling from offsite. As a cooling cycle takes approximately 12 hours this has made it possible for every member of the team to get a full nights sleep before an experiment begins.

I created simulations to find the optimal arrangement of diodes on the heavy ion detector.

Skills Used: Circuit design and analysis, PCB design, Use of Ocsilliscopes, C++, Python, academic writing, experiment design, signal processing

Jan.-Apr. 2017,

Sept.-Dec. 2017, Vancouver BC.

Co-Founder (and Exec) of MNS Society:

University of Waterloo

Materials and Nanoscience is a relatively new and challenging program at the University of Waterloo. I was lucky to have had excellent peers and mentors when I joined the program and wanted to ensure that future MNS students had the same opportunities I had.

In order to ensure there would always be a place where future MNS students could find acedemic support, friends, and fun lab events I co-founded the MNS Society
